About

Marina took her first viola lessons at the Edward Said National Conservatory of Music at the age of 11. After graduating from high school, Marina came to Germany to study at the Cologne University of Music and Dance with Professor Werner Dickel, receiving her undergraduate degree in 2017, before going to Detmold to complete her Master of Music in Prof. Diemut Poppen's class. Marina has played with the Palestine Youth Orchestra, the Mediterranean Youth Orchestra, and the Palestine National Orchestra, under conductors such as Alain Altinoglu, Gianandrea Noseda and Vincent de Kort. Marina has also taken part in various academy projects, such as the Mahler Chamber Orchestra Academy and the Schleswig-Holstein Music Festival. She has also expanded her expertise in both solo and chamber music through master classes with Nobuko Imai, Nils MÃ¶nkemeyer, Hartmut Rohde, Amandine Beyer, the Casals Quartet and the Auryn Quartet, among others. In addition to her work on the modern viola, Marina is passionate about historical interpretation. She lives out this branch of her artistic work as an orchestral and chamber musician. Marina is currently based in Cologne, participating in its diverse art and music freelance scene.
